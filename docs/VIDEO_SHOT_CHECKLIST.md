# Tap and Track Video Shot Checklist

## Format
- Length: about 2 minutes
- Style: clean screen recording with short pauses on important states
- Use: for recording the second demo option

## Before You Record
- Open the app in a browser and log out first.
- Prepare one staff account and one manager account.
- Make sure there is at least one pending attendance record to approve.
- Turn off notifications and close unrelated tabs.
- Use a large browser window so the Filament UI is readable.

## Shot List

### Shot 1: Public Homepage
Time: 0:00 - 0:12

Action:
- Open the public homepage.
- Pause on the hero section.
- Scroll slightly to show the branding and content blocks.

What to say:
- "This is the public site for the system. It presents the company and gives users a clear path to sign in."

### Shot 2: Navigation and Login Entry
Time: 0:12 - 0:20

Action:
- Move the cursor across the top navigation.
- Click Login.
- Pause briefly on the login page.

What to say:
- "The login is accessible from the public navigation, and users are redirected based on their role after sign-in."

### Shot 3: Staff Login
Time: 0:20 - 0:30

Action:
- Sign in with a staff account.
- Wait for the staff dashboard to load.
- Do not rush past the redirect.

What to say:
- "Staff land in their own dashboard where they can manage daily attendance."

### Shot 4: Clock-In Widget
Time: 0:30 - 0:55

Action:
- Focus on the clock-in widget.
- Click Get Location.
- Show that the location is captured.
- Click Clock In.
- Pause on the status change.

What to say:
- "The system captures location during clock-in and records the shift immediately."

On-screen emphasis:
- Location captured state
- Clock-in button
- Pending approval or active shift status

### Shot 5: Staff Summary Widgets
Time: 0:55 - 1:08

Action:
- Scroll to the time summary cards.
- Show today, this week, and this month totals.
- Open the attendance logs page if available in the sidebar.

What to say:
- "Staff can see worked time, overtime, and shift history in one place."

### Shot 6: Manager Login
Time: 1:08 - 1:18

Action:
- Log out.
- Sign in with a manager account.
- Wait for the manager panel to load.

What to say:
- "Managers get access to team-level attendance tools and approval workflows."

### Shot 7: Approval Queue
Time: 1:18 - 1:38

Action:
- Open Clock-In Approvals.
- Select a pending record.
- Add an approval note if you want to show that field.
- Click Approve or Reject.

What to say:
- "Pending and temporary records can be reviewed with notes, which keeps the process auditable."

On-screen emphasis:
- Pending approval status
- Approval notes
- Success notification after action

### Shot 8: Manager Overview
Time: 1:38 - 1:50

Action:
- Open Staff Attendance Overview.
- Show the filters and table.
- Highlight the duration and overtime columns.

What to say:
- "Managers can review staff attendance, filter records, and see work duration and overtime."

### Shot 9: Admin Attendance Logs and Export
Time: 1:50 - 2:05

Action:
- Switch to an admin account if needed.
- Open Attendance Logs.
- Show the status filter and date columns.
- Trigger CSV export or open the print view.

What to say:
- "Admins have full reporting access, including export and print options for attendance records."

### Shot 10: Ending Frame
Time: 2:05 - 2:10

Action:
- Return to the homepage or a clean dashboard view.
- Hold on a final static frame.

What to say:
- "One system for attendance capture, approval, and reporting across all roles."

## Recording Tips
- Keep mouse movement slow and deliberate.
- Leave a one-second pause after each major click.
- If geolocation fails, use a test environment or manually configured coordinates.
- Use real sample data so the approval queue looks believable.
- If a screen is crowded, zoom the browser to 90% instead of cropping too much.

## Suggested End Card Text
Role-based attendance tracking
Location verification
Manager approvals
Exportable reports
